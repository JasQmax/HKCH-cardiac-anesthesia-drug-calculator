📋 PALS 2025 Cardiac Arrest Algorithm

⚡ SHOCKABLE
VF / Pulseless VT
  1. Defibrillate IMMEDIATELY — rapid defibrillation is the priority:
    2 J/kg = — J
    ⚠️ If defibrillation not immediately possible, give Adrenaline first
  2. Resume CPR immediately after shock — do not pause to check rhythm
  3. 2 min CPR → Check rhythm → if still shockable:
  4. Defibrillate — 2nd shock, then give Adrenaline, then resume CPR:
    4 J/kg = — J Adrenaline 10 mcg/kg = — mcg → — mL
    Conc: —
  5. 2 min CPR → Check rhythm → if still shockable:
  6. Defibrillate — 3rd shock, then give Amiodarone OR Lignocaine (1st dose), then resume CPR:
    4 J/kg = — J
    Amio 5 mg/kg = — mg (— mL) 50 mg/mL — dilute 1:1 D5W for peripheral IV
    Ligno 1st dose (1 mg/kg) = — mg (— mL) 20 mg/mL (2% solution)
  7. Subsequent shocks + repeat doses — continue shock → drug → CPR cycles:
    Shock energy: ≥ 4 J/kg — may escalate; max 10 J/kg or adult dose (360 J)
    Amiodarone repeat: 5 mg/kg (max 150 mg) = — mg (— mL) — may repeat up to 3 doses total
    Lignocaine 2nd dose: 0.5–0.75 mg/kg after 5–10 min = — mg (— mL) 20 mg/mL
  8. Treat reversible causes (4H + 4T)
🫀 NON-SHOCKABLE
PEA / Asystole
  1. CPR 2 min — high-quality, minimise interruptions
  2. IV/IO access — Adrenaline ASAP, target within 5 min of arrest:
    10 mcg/kg = — mcg → — mL
    Conc: —
    ⏱️ Earlier adrenaline → improved outcomes
  3. Continue CPR 2 min → Check rhythm
  4. If still non-shockable: repeat Adrenaline every 3–5 min
  5. Treat reversible causes (4H + 4T)
  6. Consider advanced airway
No shock for PEA/Asystole. Focus on high-quality CPR, early adrenaline, and reversible causes.
🔍 Reversible Causes — 4H & 4T
4 H
  • Hypoxia
  • Hypovolaemia
  • Hypo/Hyperkalaemia & metabolic
  • Hypothermia
4 T
  • Thrombosis (pulmonary / coronary)
  • Tension pneumothorax
  • Tamponade (cardiac)
  • Toxins / drugs
✅ Post-ROSC Care
  • Avoid hypoxia (target SpO₂ 94–99%)
  • Avoid hyperventilation — target normal PaCO₂
  • 12-lead ECG; treat reversible cause
  • Glucose management (avoid hypo/hyperglycaemia)
  • Temperature: Prevent hyperthermia — maintain central temperature < 37.5 °C. Avoid hyperthermia strictly in comatose infants and children to improve neurological outcomes. Active cooling if needed; continuous temperature monitoring.
  • Haemodynamic targets: Maintain systolic BP and MAP > 10th percentile for age and sex. Use IV fluids and vasopressors/inotropes as needed to achieve and sustain these targets.

🫀 CPR Quality & Haemodynamic Targets
📌 CPR Technique
  • Push hard — ≥ ⅓ AP chest diameter
  • Push fast — 100–120 compressions/min
  • Allow full chest recoil
  • Minimise interruptions — < 10 sec
  • Rotate compressors every 2 min
🔢 Compression : Ventilation Ratio
  • No advanced airway:
    • 15:2 — 2 rescuers, prepuberty
    • 30:2 — 2 rescuers, post-puberty
    • 30:2 — 1 rescuer, any age
  • Advanced airway in place:
    • Continuous compressions
    • 1 breath every 2–3 sec (20–30/min)
📊 Haemodynamic Targets (invasive monitoring)
  • Diastolic BP ≥ 25 mmHg — infants
  • Diastolic BP ≥ 30 mmHg — children ≥ 1 yr
  • Use to guide CPR quality & vasopressor titration
Reference: AHA/PALS 2025 Guidelines — Pediatric Advanced Life Support.

⚠️ Key Points:
  • Amiodarone & Procainamide: Do not use together (risk of prolonged QT / hypotension)
  • Amiodarone: Dilute in D5W; for arrest give as rapid bolus; for stable arrhythmia infuse over 20–60 min
  • Adenosine: Give as very rapid IV push into the most proximal vein, immediately followed by 5–10 mL NS flush
  • Atropine: Avoid doses < 0.1 mg (paradoxical bradycardia). Not first-line for hypothermia-induced bradycardia
  • Lignocaine: Slow IV bolus over 2–3 min; monitor for CNS toxicity (seizures, perioral tingling)

⚠️ Usage Notes — Rapid Oxygen Insufflation:
  • Start at a slow respiratory rate of 15/min with an I:E ratio of 1:2 to 1:3
  • Flow rate should be titrated to minimal chest rise — cease insufflation once mild chest excursion is observed
  • Allow adequate time for expiration after each insufflation

  • The next insufflation should not begin until the chest has fully fallen and SpO₂ begins to decline
  • Target SpO₂: 80–90% — avoid over-oxygenation; insufflation should be titrated to maintain SpO₂ within this range

💉 Intraoperative IV Management
  • First line: D10% 2 mL/kg IV over 5–10 min (max 25 mL)
  • If D10% unavailable: D50% 0.5 g/kg — dilute 1:4 with NS before giving slowly
  • Follow immediately with D10% maintenance infusion (GIR 4–6 mg/kg/min)
  • Recheck BSL in 15–30 min; repeat bolus once if not improving
  • Refractory: increase GIR stepwise (up to 10–12 mg/kg/min); hydrocortisone 2–5 mg/kg IV if adrenal insufficiency suspected; consult paediatrician / intensivist
  • Target BSL ≥3.3 mmol/L (neonates ≥2.6 mmol/L)

💉 Insulin infusion preparation (1 unit/mL):
Draw 0.5 mL Actrapid (100 IU/mL) + 49.5 mL 0.9% NaCl → 50 mL total at 1 unit/mL
Prime infusion line before connecting to patient
🚨 Suspect DKA if ALL of:
  • BSL >11 mmol/L
  • pH <7.3 or bicarbonate <15 mmol/L
  • Ketonaemia (>3 mmol/L) or ketonuria
  • Unexplained metabolic acidosis

⚖️ Ideal Body Weight (IBW)

Ideal Body Weight is the weight corresponding to the 50th percentile for the patient's height and sex.
Used for drug dosing in obese patients where volume of distribution correlates with lean/ideal mass (e.g. some anaesthetic agents).
Ideal Body Weight
—
kg (50th percentile for height)
Formula used
—
📋 IBW Formulae & References
Children (2–10 years): IBW (kg) = Age (years) × 2 + 8
Adolescents / Adults — Male (Devine): IBW (kg) = 50 + 2.3 × [Height (inches) − 60]
Adolescents / Adults — Female (Devine): IBW (kg) = 45.5 + 2.3 × [Height (inches) − 60]
For children < 2 years, IBW is less clinically useful; actual weight or length-based dosing is preferred.
References:
  1. Devine BJ. Gentamicin therapy. Drug Intell Clin Pharm. 1974;8:650–655. [Original Devine formula]
  2. Traub SL, Johnson CE. Comparison of methods of estimating creatinine clearance in children. Am J Hosp Pharm. 1980;37(2):195–201. [Paediatric IBW formula: Age × 2 + 8]
  3. Hall JE. Guyton and Hall Textbook of Medical Physiology. 13th ed. Elsevier; 2016.

🧮 Adjusted Body Weight (ABW)

Adjusted Body Weight accounts for the partial distribution of some drugs into excess adipose tissue.
ABW = IBW + 0.4 × (Actual Body Weight − IBW). Used when actual weight > 120% of IBW.
